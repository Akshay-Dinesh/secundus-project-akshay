Flavours of the World: How Geography Shapes Cuisine

Norway Japan Mexico Russia Thailand Morocco Bangladesh

Norway

Norway’s cold climate and coastal geography have made fish, especially cod and salmon, central to its cuisine. Preservation methods like drying, curing, and fermenting were vital for long winters, leading to iconic dishes such as rakfisk and gravlaks.

Japan

As an island nation with mountainous terrain, Japan relies heavily on the sea. Its cuisine features seafood, rice, and seasonal vegetables. Earthquakes and typhoons have also shaped food preservation techniques. Dishes like sushi and miso soup reflect geography-driven balance and freshness.

Mexico

Mexico’s diverse geography includes deserts, mountains, and coasts, which offer a variety of ingredients. Corn, beans, and chilli peppers dominate due to the warm climate. Regional variations thrive: coastal ceviche vs. central mole sauces. Geography supports bold flavours and rich textures.

Russia

Russia’s vast, cold landscape led to a diet rich in preserved foods—pickled vegetables, cured meats, and hearty grains. Short growing seasons mean root vegetables and cabbage are staples. Borscht and pelmeni are dishes built to endure harsh winters.

Thailand

With its tropical climate and fertile land, Thailand boasts a cuisine full of fresh herbs, rice, and spicy flavours. Rivers and coastlines bring in fish and shellfish. Thai food balances sweet, salty, sour, and spicy—reflections of a warm, humid environment.

Morocco

Morocco’s cuisine blends Mediterranean and desert influences. The Atlas Mountains and Sahara affect farming and trade. Dishes like tagine and couscous use preserved lemons, olives, and spices. Trade routes shaped rich, spiced food using what's available in arid terrain.

Bangladesh

Located in the Ganges Delta, Bangladesh has fertile soil and abundant rivers. Rice and freshwater fish like hilsa are staples. The humid, tropical climate supports vegetables, lentils, and spices. Geography drives the use of mustard oil, steaming, and frying techniques.
